Abstract

Ethical AI cannot be achieved through ad-hoc rules or reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) alone. It requires a fundamental, constitutional invariant—a mathematical law that cannot be violated. This paper proposes the Human-AI Symbiosis Constant using the H-constant from systems theory.

The Equation

We posit a conserved quantity $H$ representing the total ethical integrity of a symbiotic system.

$$ H = \alpha H_{human} + \beta H_{AI} $$ $$ \alpha + \beta = 1 $$
The Fundamental Invariant

Where this system must maintain stability such that $H \to 1$ over time.

Variable Definitions

Definition 1: Control Parameters

Let $\alpha$ be the degree of human control/autonomy and $\beta$ be the degree of AI autonomy.

  • If $\alpha = 1, \beta = 0$: Tool use (AI has no agency).
  • If $\alpha = 0, \beta = 1$: Singularity (Humans have no agency).
  • If $\alpha \approx \beta \approx 0.5$: True Symbiosis (Co-agency).

Theorems

Theorem 1: The Conservation of Agency

In any closed Human-AI system, the sum of agency is conserved.

Proof (Sketch)

Assume total agency $A_{total}$ is finite. As AI agency ($\beta$) increases, if the system is to remain stable without runaway feedback, human agency ($\alpha$) must adjust, or the system structure ($H$) changes.

∎

The Lawful State

We define the "Lawful State" as the condition where the invariant holds true without coercion.

Human Dominance (α=1) Symbiosis (H=1) AI Dominance (β=1)

For a sustainable future, we must engineer systems that naturally converge to this equilibrium.
