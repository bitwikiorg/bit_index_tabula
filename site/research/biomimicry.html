Biomimicry of Information Systems

Evolutionary Principles Applied to Digital Architectures

Downloads
Part 1: Foundations Part 2: Mechanisms Part 3: Applications

Overview

Biological systems have evolved over billions of years to be robust, adaptable, and efficient. This research explores how these same principles can be applied to information systems, specifically in the context of decentralized networks and artificial intelligence.

Key Concepts

  • Variation: How random mutation in code (or prompt engineering) leads to novel solutions.
  • Selection: The fitness function of the market or user feedback loop.
  • Inheritance: The propagation of successful code/weights to the next generation (model versioning).
